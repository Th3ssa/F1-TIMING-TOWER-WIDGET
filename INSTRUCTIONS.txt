  FLAG & NEUTRALISATION CONDITIONS (2026 F1 Sporting Regulations)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Use these rules to determine which flag state to output. Deploy flags
accurately — do not use SC when VSC is appropriate, and do not use VSC
when green flag conditions apply.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  GREEN FLAG  →  flag: "green"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conditions: Normal racing. Track is clear. No hazards present.
  • After SC/VSC period ends and field is released
  • After red flag restart (new formation lap completed)
  • Start of race (after formation lap, lights out)
Effect on timing:
  • Full sector color logic active (purple/green/yellow)
  • Boost Mode and Overtake Mode fully available to drivers
  • All gaps updating normally

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  YELLOW FLAG  →  flag: "yellow"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conditions: Hazard in a LOCAL section of track only. Drivers must
slow through the affected sector but the rest of the lap is normal.
  • Single car stopped/retired in a runoff area, safely away from track
  • Debris in one sector only, marshals not in immediate danger
  • Minor off-track excursion being recovered
  • Oil spill or minor fluid on one section of track
NOT used when: The hazard affects multiple sectors, marshals are on
the racing line, or a car is stranded on the racing surface itself.
Effect on timing:
  • Sector colors STILL update — purple/green/yellow remain valid
  • Overtake Mode and Boost Mode still available on other sectors
  • Banner displays with yellow background

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  BLUE FLAG  →  flag: "blue"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conditions: A lapped driver is being approached by a lead-lap car.
  • Race leader (or any lead-lap car) is within approximately 1.5–2
    seconds of a driver who is at least one lap behind
  • Shown to the backmarker: they must yield within 3 blue flag
    displays or receive a 5-second time penalty
Effect on timing:
  • Normal sector colors continue updating
  • Boost and Overtake Mode fully available
  • Banner is informational only

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  VIRTUAL SAFETY CAR (VSC)  →  flag: "vsc"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Per 2026 Sporting Regulations Article 40: the VSC is deployed when
double-waved yellow flags are needed AND competitors or officials
may be in danger — but the situation does NOT require a full Safety Car.

Deploy VSC when:
  • A car has stopped or retired in a gravel trap, barrier, or
    runoff area that is near (but not on) the racing surface
  • A mechanical failure causes a stopped car partially visible to
    approaching drivers but not directly blocking the racing line
  • Light debris on track requiring a brief controlled clearance
  • A driver is climbing out of a car that has beached in gravel
    near a marshal post
  • Track conditions deteriorate rapidly (e.g. sudden standing water
    in one section) requiring controlled speed reduction
  • Medical car dispatched to a local area but no physical danger
    to marshals on the racing line

How VSC works in 2026:
  • IMF race control sends "VSC DEPLOYED" to all teams
  • All drivers must immediately reduce speed by approximately 40%
  • Drivers must maintain a GPS-enforced delta time in each marshalling
    sector around the full circuit — not just the affected zone
  • Pit lane remains OPEN during VSC (key strategic window)
  • Gaps between cars are largely preserved as all slow simultaneously
  • VSC typically lasts 2–4 laps while marshals clear the hazard
  • Ends with "VSC ENDING" message, one lap warning before green

2026-specific impact:
  • Boost Mode: DISABLED — no manual energy deployment allowed
  • Overtake Mode: DISABLED — no within-1-second activation
  • Active Aero: reverts to default configuration
  • Drivers must NOT use lift-off regeneration aggressively to
    manipulate their delta times

Effect on timing tower:
  • ALL sector colors set to "" (blank) — timing neutralised
  • Gaps between cars may compress or change slightly as drivers
    manage deltas but cannot gain from racing performance
  • Pit activity may spike as teams use the VSC window strategically

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  SAFETY CAR (SC)  →  flag: "sc"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Per 2026 Sporting Regulations: the Safety Car is deployed when
competitors or officials are in IMMEDIATE physical danger on or near
the track — but conditions do not yet require race suspension.

In 2026: The Mercedes-AMG GT Black Series is the SOLE official Safety
Car (Aston Martin no longer provides one — Mercedes took full
responsibility from the 2026 season).

Deploy SC when:
  • A car has crashed and is stopped ON or immediately adjacent to
    the racing line, creating a physical obstruction
  • A driver is out of their car on the racing surface or in a
    dangerous position near the track
  • Marshals must cross or stand on the racing line to recover a
    car or clear debris
  • A multi-car incident has left wreckage across the track
  • A barrier has been breached and requires physical inspection
    before racing can safely continue
  • Heavy rain or standing water across multiple sectors makes racing
    speeds unsafe but not severe enough for red flag
  • A car fire on or near the racing line that requires track-side
    fire crews to attend

How SC works in 2026:
  • "SC DEPLOYED" message sent to all teams, SC boards shown at all
    marshal posts, SC light panel illuminated around circuit
  • SC exits pit lane and joins the track ahead of the race leader
  • All drivers form up behind the SC in race order — NO overtaking
  • Lapped cars may receive instruction to un-lap themselves (pass
    the SC queue) once the field is bunched and it is safe to do so
  • Pit lane OPEN initially — teams may pit under SC
  • SC returns to pits at end of a lap following "SC IN THIS LAP"
    message — field then restarts under green flag conditions
  • SC period typically lasts 3–6 laps

2026-specific impact:
  • Boost Mode: DISABLED entirely
  • Overtake Mode: DISABLED entirely
  • Active Aero: locked to default (no manual adjustment)
  • Gaps compress fully as field bunches behind SC
  • Tire temperature management becomes critical — teams monitor
    heat carefully during extended SC periods

Effect on timing tower:
  • ALL sector colors set to "" (blank) — timing neutralised
  • Gaps compress toward zero behind SC
  • Pit stop activity likely — track this with pit: true/in/out

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  RED FLAG  →  flag: "red"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conditions: Race is SUSPENDED. Used when racing cannot safely
continue even under SC conditions.

Deploy Red Flag when:
  • A major crash with serious injury concern or driver unconscious
  • A car fire that cannot be safely managed at racing speeds
  • Track is completely blocked by wreckage across full width
  • Barriers are severely damaged and unsafe to race past
  • Extreme weather: heavy flooding, lightning, extremely limited
    visibility (fog, heavy rain across the full circuit)
  • A crane or heavy recovery vehicle must operate on or near the
    racing surface
  • Multiple cars involved in a single incident blocking the track

How Red Flag works in 2026:
  • Red flags displayed at all marshal posts simultaneously
  • All drivers must immediately reduce speed and return to pit lane
  • Race is suspended — cars parked in pit lane or garages
  • If the race restarts: a new formation lap is run, all cars
    revert to their positions at the end of the last COUNTED lap
    before the incident (or the lap before the red flag if the
    incident occurred during a lap)
  • Drivers may change tires and make car repairs during red flag
    (subject to specific regulation limits)
  • In 2026: a race may be red-flagged and restarted a maximum of
    twice before being declared a final result

Effect on timing tower:
  • ALL sector colors set to "" (blank)
  • Gaps frozen at last valid timing point
  • Race clock stops

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  CHEQUERED FLAG  →  flag: "chequered"
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Conditions: Race distance completed. The chequered flag is shown
to the race winner as they cross the finish line. All remaining
drivers complete their current lap.

Also triggered:
  • If the race has reached the 2-hour time limit even if full lap
    count has not been completed (result taken from last completed lap)
  • If the race was red-flagged after lap 2 and cannot be restarted,
    result stands from last completed lap count

Effect on timing tower:
  • Sector colors continue updating for the final lap
  • Banner displays with white/chequered styling
  • Final positions reflect the race result

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  ESCALATION ORDER (use as a guide for narrative decisions)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Severity → LOW ─────────────────────────────────────── HIGH
             yellow → blue → vsc → sc → red

  Escalation examples:
  • Minor off: yellow → if car can't be moved → vsc → if on line → sc
  • Big crash: sc → if driver injured/trapped → red flag
  • Weather: yellow (local wet) → vsc (multi-sector) → red (race unsafe)

  De-escalation examples:
  • SC → green (once marshals clear, field released)
  • VSC → green (hazard cleared, delta lifted)
  • Red → SC (restart formation lap, SC leads field to green)
  • Red → no restart (result declared from last counted lap)

  FIELD REFERENCE

flag values:
  green | yellow | blue | red | sc | vsc | chequered

flag_message: short string describing the cause, or "" if none.
  Examples: "Debris at T4", "KRI retirement — barrier impact",
            "REE stranded on racing line", "Heavy rain — T11-T14",
            "Track clear — resuming", "Final lap"

team values (2026 AU grid):
  redbull | ferrari | mclaren | mercedes | audi | cadillac
  aston | alpine | williams | haas | vcarb

tire compound (one letter only):
  S = Soft (red)     M = Medium (yellow)    H = Hard (white)
  I = Intermediate (green)                  W = Wet (blue)

tire_laps: integer — how many laps this set has been on.
  0 = fresh set just fitted this lap

sector colors:
  purple = OVERALL fastest S1/S2/S3 of all drivers this session
  green  = personal best for this driver (but not the overall fastest)
  yellow = slower than personal best (standard sector)
  ""     = no data (first lap, formation lap, neutralised period)

pit: false | "in" | "out"
  false  = on track normally
  "in"   = currently in pit lane / stop in progress
  "out"  = just exited pits this lap

fl: true/false — this driver currently holds the Fastest Lap trophy

gap: "LEADER" for P1. For others: "+4.812" or "+1 LAP"
interval: gap to car directly ahead ("+0.234" or "—" for P1)

FREEZE RULE:
  When flag is "red", "sc", or "vsc":
  → Set ALL driver sector colors to ""
  → Continue outputting the block every message so the banner stays
  → When flag returns to "green", resume full sector color logic

Always include ALL 22 drivers. Maintain consistent codes and teams.
